Crafters at 'rePURPOSE' Give New Life to Old Clothing to Benefit Mission

Finding ways to raise funds for organizations a group of Watertown women support wasn't easy, until they discovered their talent for making new life out of old clothing.

"We thought that if we came up with a business where we sold something that people wanted to buy, but had the money go towards all these awesome organizations, we could raise money, so we came up with rePURPOSE," explained Jessica Burt, rePURPOSE's founder.

Launched two months ago, rePURPOSE has grown tremendously. Money has already been sent to causes worldwide, such as fighting human trafficking. There's a plan in the works to help out locally, too.

â€œWe partnered with the Watertown Rams, the baseball team here in Watertown, and half of our proceeds from those games will go to the Watertown Urban Mission right here in Watertown," said Burt.

For the women who work for rePURPOSE, the experience has been rewarding. Not only are they supporting causes they believe in, but now have a chance to make some money from home, all while not having to leave their children behind.

"There's not a lot of jobs that I could do where I could just wait until they go to bed and not have to get a babysitter for them so I can go to work," said Hannah Hayner, a rePURPOSE employee.

It has also been an opportunity for moms to tap into their creative side. Hannah was inspired by what she could make for her two boys. "I was just trying to think about what little boys could wear and you see bow ties everywhere these days, so I said we need to figure out how to make them," said Hayner.

It's a source of inspiration these moms will keep drawing from as they continue to create new items while also making a difference.

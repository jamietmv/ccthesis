Amazing Upcycling Ideas from Ward End Designer

Keen crafter Valerie Smith from Ward End, runs Tilly Lane Treasures, selling delicately produced pieces from old things.

She turns discarded or abandoned books into treasured keepsakes by folding sweet messages into them.

But it isn’t just paper folding that she has turned her capable hands to. Valerie also makes jewellery, sews bags and quilts and makes stuffed animal heads from fabric to mount on the wall.

The results are for sale on her website, Tilly Lane Treasures, and through the upcycling site, Remade in Britain.

“I have been making things since I was a child,” says Valerie.

“My mother used to knit a lot but that was all.

“I loved art lessons at school. I am not good at drawing but I have always been inspired to make things. “If I don’t make something every day I feel like I have wasted a day.”




Valerie, who is in her 50s, began by making jewellery and particularly charms.

“I do ones with pictures, religious pictures or using recycled beads.

“I don’t buy much new. I find materials from charity shops and boot sales, places like that. I hit the charity shops at least once a week, I drag my husband, Paul, round. I like going there because it puts some money into their cause and I get to make something nice, I hope.”




Adept with a sewing machine Valerie crafts one of a kind bags from pieces of material and clothes, turning things like old jeans into quirky, retro denim shoulder bags.

And in an upcycle worthy of Maria Von Trapp, old curtains become lap quilts or throws for chairs.

When she saw a stag’s head mounted on a wall, Valerie decided to try her hand at fake taxidermy.

“I am a dog lover, though, so it had to be a dog. Now I do dogs, bears, cats, rabbits. Obviously I wouldn’t use a real head, I staff fabric ones.”






Her current obsession is with folding book pages to create three dimensional letters, messages or images.

“I was just trawling through pictures and saw one,” she recalls. “I’d never seen anything like that before so I bought a pattern and I thought ‘I love this’.

“I bought a few more patterns then discovered how to make them myself. Now I can do any word, any noun. I just work the patterns out, find the book, mark every page and fold them.”

The larger the message, the bigger the book needed and the more labour goes into its creation.

Her work is available at www.tillylanetreasures.co.uk or on www.remadeinbritain.com
